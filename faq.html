About our Research Project and Product

1. What is Sentiment Analysis?

Opinion mining (sometimes known as sentiment analysis or emotion AI) refers to the use of natural language processing, text analysis, computational linguistics, and biometrics to systematically identify, extract, quantify, and study affective states and subjective information. Generally speaking, sentiment analysis aims to determine the attitude of a speaker, writer, or other subject with respect to some topic or the overall contextual polarity or emotional reaction to a document, interaction, or event. The attitude may be a judgment or evaluation (see appraisal theory), affective state (that is to say, the emotional state of the author or speaker), or the intended emotional communication (that is to say, the emotional effect intended by the author or interlocutor).

2. Why do we use social media for Sentiment prediction?

With the surge of online networking, the data about open emotions has become widely available. Online networking is changing how people share their opinions and feelings around subject, and with such has a significant effect on collective opinion.

3. Why do we use Twitter feeds to collect information?

Twitter, a social media platform, has received a lot of attention from researchers in the recent times. Twitter is a micro-blogging application that allows users to follow and comment other user’s thoughts or share their opinions in real time. More than 300 Million users post over 500 million tweets per day. Making Twitter like a database with valuable data for researchers. Each tweet is of 140 characters long and speaks public opinion on a topic concisely. The information exploited from tweets are very useful for making predictions.

4. How do we track sentiment and process the information?

Twitter streams arrive as text-based, unstructured, qualitative data. Their main feature is that they are non-numeric, and thus do not facilitate quick data processing the way that quantitative data do. However, Tweets may contain information about the effect and possible causes of an event. We use Google Cloud Natural Language (GCNL) which reveals the structure and meaning of text by offering powerful machine learning models in an easy to use REST API. With GCNL we can extract information about people, places, events and much more, mentioned in tweets.

5. Why is sentiment relevant for cryptocurrency trading?

Given the uncertainty and volatility amongst the cryptomarkets social media can prove to be an effective source to determine user option and to therefore extrapolate future demand. Sentiment gives investors an edge because they can see the whole picture – how the market is ‘feeling’ about a particular cryptocurrency. By using social media and news investors can gauge public opinion as a proxy for product demand and therefore effectively risk manage and trade their cryptocurrencies.
